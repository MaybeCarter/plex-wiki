I have a feeling these newsletters might become fewer and farther between. Not because I don't enjoy writing them, but because Hyperdrive has reached a point where it runs reliably without much input from me. Over the past month I've been really happy with the features I've built out, and things just haven't needed the same level of tinkering.

I'll continue sending updates when I roll out new features or make structural changes, but most of the systems and automations have stabilized. At this point, most of my work is quiet backend upkeep rather than big, visible updates.

That said, I do want to highlight the newest addition to the server: the Unwatched Requests collection. These have been live for a little while, but you may not have noticed them yet. When you open a library (either Movies or TV Shows) and switch to the Collections tab, you'll see a personalized collection at the front called {Name}'s Unwatched Requests.

The name is based on whatever you've set in Plex:

  • If you have a Full Name saved in Plex settings, the collection will use that (e.g., Carter Sanderson's Unwatched Requests).
  • If you don't, it will fall back to your Plex username (e.g., carters123's Unwatched Requests).

If you'd prefer the collection to display your actual name instead of your username, just go to Plex Settings → Account → Full Name, add your name, and save. The collection title won't change instantly, as these collections sync every 12 hours.

As you can probably guess, the Unwatched Requests collection shows everything you've requested on Jellyseerr, minus anything you've already watched. It should make it easier to find that movie you asked for a few days ago, or to resurface a show you requested a while back and never got around to watching. I've been mulling over this idea for a while, and it was fun to see it come to life.

Speaking of Jellyseerr, the teams behind Jellyseerr and Overseerr are officially merging their projects into a new unified app called Seerr. This will bring everything under one codebase, which should mean faster development, better stability, and quicker access to new features going forward. This doesn't require any action from you! I'll handle the transition once Seerr reaches a stable release, but I wanted to give you a heads-up about some exciting changes on the horizon.
