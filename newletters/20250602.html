Recent Updates to Your Favorite Plex Server

Greetings all!

If you've visited Overseerr since last night, you likely found yourself signed out and looking at a Jellyseerr login page where where Overseerr used to be. Don't worry! I've made a behind-the-scenes change and replaced Overseerr with Jellyseerr. It looks and feels nearly identical (because it's actually a fork of Overseerr), but this swap provides a platform that's more actively developed and paves the way for future enhancements, like smarter request handling and improved notifications. For context, Jellyseerr has had 14 updates in the past 365 days. Overseerr has had only one update in the last 675 days.

The swap required a database migration, which I believe I performed successfully. As far as I can tell, your accounts, requests, and settings have all been migrated safely. And if you had web push notifications enabled, they should continue to work with no action required from you. However, if you run into any quirks or unexpected behavior, please reach out and let me know. And in case you run into any showstopping issues, I left Overseerr running at overseerr-legacy.cartersanderson.com so you can still submit requests until I can resolve any potential bugs. If nobody experiences any issues, I'll deprecate Overseerr (the service, not the URL, you don't have to worry about updating your bookmarks) in the coming weeks.

Optional Cleanup (Purely Cosmetic)

Again, everything should continue to work exactly the same without you lifting a finger. However, if you're a purist (like me), you can delete the Overseerr app from your Home Screen and add the Jellyseerr app in its place:

  1. Go to jellyseerr.cartersanderson.com in Safari.
  2. Tap the "Share" icon
  3. Tap "Add to Home Screen"
  4. Ensure that web push notifications are enabled and working

It bears repeating that this is purely cosmetic and will have no effect on your usage or the behavior of the app. The only real benefit here is a new (cute!) app icon for Jellyseerr. I don't think the Overseerr icon will change on its own despite the update.

Anyway, this email is already too long for a change that has virtually no impact on your experience. As always, thanks for putting up with my constant tinkering.
