Recent Updates to Your Favorite Plex Server

Plex recently announced some big changes that start rolling out at the end of April. I wanted to get ahead of it and clear up what actually applies to Hyperdrive users — especially so you don’t accidentally pay for something you don’t need.

Remote Watch Pass? You Don’t Need It.

Starting April 29, 2025, Plex will require either a Plex Pass or a Remote Watch Pass to stream from a server when you’re not on the same Wi-Fi network.

But here’s the good news: you’re already covered.
Because I have a Lifetime Plex Pass, you can continue streaming remotely from Hyperdrive just like always — no extra subscription, no changes on your end.

Plex Pass Prices Are Going Up

Plex is also raising the price of their subscription plans:

  • Monthly: $4.99 → $6.99
  • Annual: $39.99 → $69.99
  • Lifetime: $119.99 → $249.99

Like I said, you don’t need a Plex Pass to use Hyperdrive, but if you’re interested in perks like:

  • Skip Intro + Skip Credits
  • Pass Out Protection (auto-pauses when you fall asleep)
  • Rewind on Resume
  • Download to Mobile
  • Trailers and Extras

…it might be worth locking in the old price before April 29. Totally optional, but something to consider.

More on Plex Pass

Mobile Streaming Just Got Easier

If you’ve ever been annoyed by the $5 mobile unlock fee Plex charges for watching content on your phone — good news: that fee is going away.

You can now stream from the Plex app on iOS or Android without paying anything to unlock playback. So if you’ve been holding out, this is your sign to dive in.

Plex App Redesign and User Feedback

Plex is rolling out a significant redesign of its mobile app for iOS and Android (with a TV app redesign coming soon). While the update introduces features like improved navigation and a dedicated media library tab, many users have reported issues and missing functionality, including:

  • Inability to connect to servers via manual IP address
  • App failing to load additional titles/posters in larger libraries
  • “Up Next” counter randomly skipping episodes during TV playback
  • Playback info overlay and menu missing
  • Inability to mark TV episodes as watched/unwatched or play from the Season screen
  • No ability to select which version to play when multiple are available
  • No option to pre-select audio tracks or quality before playback

...and much more.

Just a heads-up: these changes are made by Plex directly. I don’t have any control over how the app functions or looks — though I share your frustrations.

In addition to personal media servers (like Hyperdrive), Plex also offers FAST (free, ad-supported television) content and discovery features. You're welcome to keep those enabled, but I find they add clutter and make browsing more confusing.

If you'd prefer to keep the focus solely on Hyperdrive’s content, here’s how to remove the On Demand and Discover tabs from the app's bottom navigation:

How to Clean Up Your Plex App:

  1. Go into your server settings (this must be done via the Plex web interface — not the app)
  2. Click on Online Media Sources
  3. Set Movies & Shows to Disabled — this removes the On Demand tab
  4. Set Discover Source to Disabled — this removes the Discover tab

Doing this should streamline your app and make browsing Hyperdrive feel a lot cleaner.

I hope this helps you navigate the upcoming changes. As always, if you have any questions or need help, feel free to reach out.
