Greetings all!

After about a month of experimenting with poster overlays, I’ve landed on a combination I find to be helpful but not overwhelming. A few changes to highlight since last time:

  • Removed Recently Added: There’s already a “Recently Added” section on both the Home page and the Recommended page of each library, making them redundant at best and distracting at worst.
  • Added Emmy Winners: Easily identify shows that won a Primetime Emmy Award.
  • Added Streaming-Service Original: A small streaming service logo now shows which platform originally made or branded the title — e.g., a Netflix Original, an Apple TV+ drama, or a Prime action flick. To browse everything from a service (original or not), use Collections.
  • Moved Trending: The addition of the streaming service banners meant that a trending streaming service original would have overlapping overlays. Centering the trending icon resolves that issue.
  • Simplified TV Show Status: While I find it extremely useful to know at a glance if a show is current or cancelled, the previous overlays were too visually heavy for my taste. I’ve removed the words, leaving only a colored strip at the bottom of the poster: blue is airing, green is returning, grey is ended, and red is cancelled.
  • Improved New Season: I made a new “New Season” overlay that I prefer the look of and no longer interferes with the show’s status overlay.

I also made a change to how posters are managed. You shouldn’t notice any difference, but it ensures that my curated posters stick around even if the video file is upgraded. Don’t worry, though: I’m still choosing posters for every title to ensure the library remains clean and consistent.

The most-watched titles on Hyperdrive in September were…

Movies: Weapons, The Fantastic Four: First Steps, and Elio

TV Shows: South Park (in first place for the third month in a row!), The Summer I Turned Pretty (for the second month in a row), and Alien: Earth

That’s it for this month! Nothing huge, but some nice refinements to improve your experience and make it easier for me to manage. Oh, and keep an eye out for a spooky season collection on your Home page later this month!

As always, I’d love your feedback, and happy streaming!

— Carter
