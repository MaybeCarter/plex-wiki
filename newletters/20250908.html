Hi all,

First and foremost, I want to bring attention to an email Plex sent out earlier today regarding a security incident:  

An unauthorized third party accessed a limited subset of customer data from one of our databases. While we quickly contained the incident, information that was accessed included emails, usernames, and securely hashed passwords.

Any account passwords that may have been accessed were securely hashed, in accordance with best practices, meaning they cannot be read by a third party.
…

We kindly request that you reset your Plex account password immediately by visiting https://plex.tv/reset. When doing so, there's a checkbox to "Sign out connected devices after password change," which we recommend you enable. This will sign you out of all your devices (including any Plex Media Server you own) for your security, and you will then need to sign back in with your new password.

If for some reason you didn’t receive the email from Plex, you can find a copy of it here. Feel free to reach out to me if you have any further questions or concerns.

With the bad news out of the way, let’s move on to something a little more fun.

You have likely noticed that Hyperdrive’s library has looked a little different over the past few weeks. I’ve started testing a system of adding overlays to movies and TV show posters to provide helpful, at-a-glance information when browsing the library:

Movies:

  • Recently Added: The movie was added to Hyperdrive within the last seven days.
  • Trending on Hyperdrive: Popular with other users on the server right now.
  • Oscars Best Picture: The film won the Academy Award for Best Picture.
  • Cannes Winner: The film won at the Cannes Film Festival.

TV Shows:

  • Recently Added (not pictured): The show was added to Hyperdrive within the last seven days.
  • New Season (not pictured): A brand-new season has just been released (or added to Hyperdrive).
  • Status: Shows are tagged as Airing, Returning, Ended, or Canceled, so you know what’s current and what’s finished.

Now, it’s possible to go overboard with these overlays and make something that’s information-dense, but visually cluttered:

I don’t think I’ve gone too far down that road, but the aesthetics of my library are important so I’d love your feedback: do the new overlays feel helpful, distracting, or somewhere in between? Let me know what you think!
